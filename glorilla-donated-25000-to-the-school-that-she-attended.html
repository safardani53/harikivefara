GloRilla Donated $25,000 To The School That She Attended

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, January 30, 2024

GloRilla, an American rapper, contributed $25,000 to her alma institution, MLK Prep, saying she felt “fortunate” to give back to her alma mater. The 22-year-old rapper, whose real name is Gloria Woods, gave $25,000 to her school, with $12,500 going for senior dues and the remaining $12,500 going to the music department. The school is in Frayser, which is in North Memphis, Tennessee. The musician, who is also from Memphis, performed her popular song, F.N.F., for the kids. She claimed that she wanted to encourage pupils at the institution and show them that their goals may come true.

In a statement, the Memphis rapper said:

“I want children to realize that they can do everything they set their minds to.” They just need to reach for the stars if they have a dream. And anything they set their minds to, whatever they labor for, they can accomplish and get.”

.MLK College Prep Principal Chiquita Perry issued the following statement:

“We are overjoyed that our former student is returning to the hallways she previously roamed.” We are incredibly proud of Ms. Woods and grateful for her community involvement, leadership, and fellowship.”

GloRilla hail from Tennessee Memphis

GloRilla revealed to Breakbeat Media in May that she is from Memphis, stating:

“My hometown is Frayser, Tennessee.” Frayser’s [a Memphis neighborhood] I’m from Memphis, too.” She also expressed pride in being one of the post-Young Dolph Memphis rappers striving to promote the city.”

In an interview with Refinery 29, she stated:

“That’s very nice. I’m truly delighted to be a part of it because it’s a new wave. “I’m honored to be one of the pioneers of the new wave.”

In a different interview, she said that she no longer resides there. She said on Revolt’s Big Facts podcast that she “doesn’t be in Memphis much longer” since there is “too much” going on. The artist seems to be referring to violence against the city’s rappers.

GloRilla’s most recent single, F.N.F, reached number one on the Billboard Mainstream R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay Chart.

Glorilla’s track F.N.F., which also featured HitKidd, became viral. On April 29, 2022, the song was uploaded and quickly went popular on the video-sharing platform TikTok, creating a breakthrough smash for both artists. On the Billboard Mainstream R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay Chart, it peaked at No. 1. Despite the fact that the song became popular on the TikTok platform, GloRilla has previously said that she is not primarily interested in making TikTok music.

In a statement to Refinery29, the rapper stated:

“I’ve got bars.” I don’t usually create TikTok songs. But sometimes it’s so difficult that it simply gets viral on there because it’s so fantastic.”

The musician has gotten appreciation from industry rappers like Travis Scott and DeJ Loaf.

GloRilla and CardiB unite on the new song Tomorrow2.

Meanwhile, GloRilla has released Tomorrow 2, a new collaboration with Cardi B that is a follow-up to her previous blockbuster single, Tomorrow. The rapper went on to elaborate on her portion by hinting at the track’s lyrics. She wrote about the song on Twitter:

“BUT THAT’S WHY I LOVE MONDAY.”

She also used a gold award and a green checkmark emoji before adding:

“It’s official, myself and my lady @iamcardib are engaged.” TOMORROW 2 IS DROPPING FRIDAY 9/23 AT MIDNIGHT… Save the below and let’s gooooooo.”

GloRilla’s F.N.F. was dubbed the “summer tune” by Cardi B. She subsequently appeared as a guest on Kay Flock’s Shake It with Dougie B and Bory300 in April, followed by Hot Sh*t in July, which included Kanye West and Lil Durk.
