Best Blusher Brushes 2019 | The Sun UK

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, September 15, 2024

BLUSHER is an essential part of make-up looks and yet so often the brushes to apply it are overlooked.

The best blush brush can pick up just the right amount of pigment to give you a natural flush and is fluffy enough to blend powders out for a non cakey finish.

You can do so much with a good blusher.

High-fashion editorial? Lashings of neon pink, like we so often see on the catwalk. Daytime look? Try a flush of shimmering peach powder across the apples of your cheeks.

Depending on your desired look, you'll need a special kind of blush brush to achieve that.

We've come a long way since the tiny flat brushes found in noughties make-up compacts.

These days make-up tools vary in density and shape, from a more concentrated tapered brush for accurate application to a large fluffy one for an all over flush.

Learn which one works best for you in our guide to the best blush brushes.

1. Morphe Blusher Brush

  • Morphe M527 Deluxe Pointed Powder Brush, £17 from Lookfantastic - buy here

The M527 brush by Morphe is a must-have tool for all things powder but especially for applying blush, as the soft bristles ensure that the product doesn't cake on and distributes evenly.

The full head with a slight taper is the typical shape of a classic blush brush, but the quality is incredibly high, especially for the price.

If you're after a vegan brush, bear in mind that this isn't - although Morphe does have a wide selection of vegan brushes for you to take a look at.

  • Morphe Elite Tapered Blush Brush, £18 from Cult Beauty - buy here

Morphe is a leader in the cosmetics game for a reason, its brushes are high quality and get the job done.

This baby has a flat head, tapered shape and more densely packed bristles, giving you a more concentrated distribution of product.

This particular brush could also be used for cream blush as well.

2. Charlotte Tilbury

  • Charlotte Tilbury Blusher Brush, £30 from House of Fraser - buy here

The gorgeous burgundy and rose gold-coloured brushes from Charlotte Tilbury are not only stunning, they work incredibly well too.

They're not cheap but they are worth every penny.

Penelope Cruz has her own personalised set which she loves.

For £30 you can grab the best-selling round, fluffy, blusher brush from House of Fraser, but be quick as this particular brush tends to sell out often.

3. Real Techniques Blusher Brush

  • Real Techniques Bold Metals Collection 300 Tapered Blush Brush, £20.40 from Escentual - buy here

This stunning rose gold brush from Real Techniques looks a bit like a magic wand, and magic is exactly what it'll work on your face.

It's a part of the slightly more luxe collection by Real Techniques, as the brand's typical price point is under £15 a brush.

However, this is reflected in the quality and style and the tapered brush makes it more concentrated for a bold flush of colour.

  • Real Techniques Blush Brush, £9.99 from Look Fantastic - buy here

This particular brush was one of the first ever that the Chapman sisters, Sam and Nic launched for Real Techniques.

It's been a huge hit for over 7 years now and is still one of their best selling tools.

A large, fluffy brush, it's a lot less dense than their rose gold model and will distribute a subtle flush which can be built up.

It's an absolute steal too at £8.40.

4. E.L.F

  • e.l.f Blush Brush, £3.80 from Feelunique - buy here

This budget brush from e.l.f is an incredibly popular brush, more so than certain ones that are ten times the price.

Don't let the low price fool you into thinking it's a poor-quality brush, the brand has not compromised on quality when constructing this clever tool.

The brush is a classic rounded shape and the entirely black design is super chic.

It's also vegan-friendly.
