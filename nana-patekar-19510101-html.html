Vishwanath Patekar, better known as Nana Patekar, is an Indian actor, writer, philanthropist and filmmaker, working primarily in Hindi and Marathi cinema. He is known for his role in the film Agni Sakshi written by Ranbir Pushp and he won the National Cinema Award for Best Supporting Actor. He won another National Cinema Award for Best Supporting Actor and the Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role in Parinda. He went on to win the Filmfare Best Villain Award for his role in Angaar. In 1995, he won the National Film Award for Best Actor as well as the Best Film and Screen Actor Award for his role in Krantiveer. He also won the Filmfare Best Villain Award for his role in Apaharan. In 2017, he won the Filmfare Marathi Award for Best Actor for his performance in Natsamrat. He is, along with Irrfan Khan, the only actor to have won the Filmfare Award in the categories of Best Actor, Best Supporting Actor and Best Villain.
