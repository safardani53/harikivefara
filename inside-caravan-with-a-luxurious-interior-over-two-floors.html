Inside the 100k caravan with a stunning interior over two floors - and its own sun terrace

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, April 20, 2024

CARAVAN holidays are usually a great way to take a break without spending too much money.

But, not all caravans are equal - and there's certainly nothing budget about the Kabe Imperial Tower, which costs around £100,000 and has two floors.

Swedish company, Kabe, produces luxury motorhomes and caravans, and its Imperial Tower creation was one of its more outlandish experiments.

The two-story home-on-wheels was actually a PR stunt first shown at the Royal Tower in 2007, but it gained so much interest that it was then considered for production.

The Imperial Tower is a twin-axle that's an incredible 9.7 metres long and 4.5 meters high.

Inside, it's fitted with luxurious leather and wood and features a comfy wraparound lounge at the front, although you can spec it in different layouts.

On the ground floor, you'll also find a dinette and a fully-fitted kitchen with a full-size cooker and sink.

At the rear is the bathroom with a walk-in shower and bedroom, which can come with twin beds or an island double.

Up a staircase on the second floor is a second lounge featuring another huge wraparound sofa.

A door then leads out onto a terrace, which can be used for dining or sunbathing in the warmer months.

Sadly, despite another stab at it in 2017, the Kabe Imperial Tower never went into production.

It's reported that a price tag of around £100,000 and stability issues due to its height ultimately stopped it.

Today Kabe offers a range of luxurious caravans called the Royal and Imperial but all are single-story vans.
