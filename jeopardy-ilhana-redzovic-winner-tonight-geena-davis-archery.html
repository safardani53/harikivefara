Jeopardy! fans stunned as contestants stumble into first commercial break without any money in extre

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, April 2, 2024

JEOPARDY! viewers were in for quite the Hump Day moment on Wednesday's episode.

All three contestants went into the first break with either $0 or negative scores, baffling fans.

Ilhana Redzovic, an investment associate from Chicago, Illinois, was the one to beat returning with a one-day total of $20,400.

She wound up beating Lisa Gargiulo, a middle school English teacher from Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania and Kyle Marshall, a library branch manager from Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.

But as the Jeopardy! round began, the contestants didn't exactly have their game faces on and just kept missing clues as Mayim Bialik, 47, held court.

There were six incorrect responses in the first 15 prompts and Ilhana landed on the round's only Daily Double.

She wagered her entire $2200, capping off the portion of the episode with, unfortunately, another miss.

It read: "Lamborghini's Sesto Elemento was named for its pioneering use of this, from which much of the car is made."

Ilhana incorrectly guessed "Aluminum" - the clue was looking for "copper" and the host then pumped the breaks for a commercial.

In quite a shocking sight, the scores at the first break were, as a result, Ilhana at $0, Kyle at -$200, and Lisa at -$200.

FANS ARE SPEECHLESS

Fans didn't go easy on the contestants, one writing on Reddit: “That was the worst Jeopardy round I've ever seen."

Another wrote: “I don't know if it was just me, but that was pretty dismal.

"Better than I could have done given the difficulty I found in today's clues, but yeah. That Jeopardy round score was pretty low across the board. It seems like everyone had trouble staying out of the negative!"

Another wondered: "When was the last time they went to first break with no one above $0?"

“These players guessed too much," joked a fourth.

"This is the worst game of #Jeopardy I’ve ever seen. Also if they don’t make Ken Jennings the host again I’m gonna lose it," tweeted another.

A fifth wrote: "This game brought to you by nobody wants to win."

"I’ve watched a lot of Jeopardy. Dunno if I’ve ever seen them go to the first break with no one having money (and two in the negative)" wrote another.

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN EVERYONE HAS $0?

Other fans wondered what happens when no one has any money at the end of the game as it aired.

According to Newsweek, all three contestants being at $0 at the end of Final Jeopardy has happened six times.

Most famously, just that happened during Alex Trebek's second-ever game hosting and the crowd was furious.

The clue read: "Calendar date with which the 20th century began" - everyone guessed Jan. 1, 1900 but the correct response was January 1, 1901.

As a result, all three players get sent home packing and there's no returning champion.

ACTRESS-TURNED-ARCHER!

Luckily, in this episode - that was not the case and things picked up in the second round.

Ilhana led going into Final Jeopardy with $10,400, Kyle with $10,200, and Lisa at $4,600.

Final Jeopardy! under "Sports and Movies" read: "A Geena Davis Institute study found shortly after a 2012 franchise film’s release, women’s participation in this sport rose 105%"

No one was correct with "archery" -a nod to the movie Hunger Games.

Ilhana chose to wager $0 from the lead, winning $10,400 for a two-day total of $30,800.

Though the clue wasn't a bullseye for the contestants, fans learned an interesting tidbit about Geena Davis, star of 1986's The Fly and A League of Their Own.
