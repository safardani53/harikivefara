TINY HOME LIVING

Homeownership of a regular house has become increasingly cost-prohibitive, causing customers to turn to other alternatives as a means to live comfortably.

Homeowners are especially being hit hard with post-closing costs.

This includes things like utility activation, down payments, HOA fees, and moving fees which can impact a homeowner's financial state for years.

Many people are taking to social media with their tiny home tales, revealing major home transformations they've made and the money they've saved along the way.

One user revealed how she converted her shed into a tiny home for just $5,000.

“This was super budget-friendly,” she told the YouTube channel Exploring Alternatives during a tour of her home.

She was able to create an office space with a desk, sofa, kitchen, and a lofted bedroom.

Meanwhile, a family in Texas transformed their farmyard shed into a tiny home for just $16,000.

After inheriting a farm, the couple turned a rundown shed on the property into a home for them and their two children.

Like many tiny home owners, they also did 90% of the work themselves.

The only thing that required outside help was the construction of the interior walls which was left up to a professional contractor.
