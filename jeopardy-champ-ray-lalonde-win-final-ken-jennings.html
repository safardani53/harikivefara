Jeopardy! fans left stunned after super-champ Ray LaLonde secures 13th straight win by just $100 on

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, April 17, 2024

THE first new Jeopardy! episode of the year was nearly the last for Ray LaLonde - and it has fans buzzing.

The multi-day champion barely held on to win Final Jeopardy! by a super small margin.

On Monday's episode, Ray was back to defend his title.

The Toronto native entered the game on a 12-day win streak, amassing a total of $354,300.

But midway through the Double Jeopardy! round, and after some failed Daily Doubles, he was down to just $1,000.

Meanwhile, Denise Carlon of Seaside Heights, New Jersey, was dominating, quickly passing the $10,000 mark.

However, after an incredible comeback, Ray finished the game in first place, with a total of $16,100.

Denise was right on his heels with $16,000 entering Final Jeopardy!

FINAL DESTINATION

"A close, close match heading into Final, with only 100 separating the leaders," host Ken Jennings teased.

The category was Historic Crimes, and the clue was: "Saying it was stolen by Napoleon, Self-styled Italian patriot Vincenzo Peruggia took it in 1911."

Denise, in second place, answered "What is the Mona Lisa?"

That was correct. She put the champ on the ropes when it was revealed she wagered everything - doubling her total to $32,000.

Finally, it was Ray's turn.

The champ also correctly guessed, "What is the Mona Lisa." And he too wagered $16,000, leaving him with $32,100.

Ken announced: "By $100 sir, you are a lucky 13-day champion."

Ray ended the day with a grand total of $386,400. Online, fans seemed as relieved as he did.

"What a game!" one viewer exclaimed.

Another added: "Whew. Thank god Ray prevailed after a weak performance in Jeopardy! and getting screwed over 2 daily doubles to win the fight."

CLOSE CALL

The close game to start the new year only mirrored his final game of 2022, in which the scenic artist barely clinched his 11th straight victory after facing stiff competition from two newbies.

Last week, the returning champ faced Rachel Cohen, a consultant from Denver, Colorado, and Emily Kawaler, a bioinformatician from New York, New York.

The game got off to a tough start for the Canadian contestant as he lost his first $3,000 by wagering it all on a true Daily Double which he incorrectly answered.

The lead flipped between Ray and Emily, as she went into Final Jeopardy! ahead at $14,200, while he had $13,200, and Rachel trailed at $11,800.

This meant that Ray was behind going into Final Jeopardy! for the first time since he joined the show.

Host Ken Jennings remarked: “This has been a fantastic game,” before revealing the category for the episode’s final clue.

Under the category “gods and goddesses”, the clue read: “Each morning she began her ride in her chariot across the sky ahead of her brother Sol, or Helios.”

DOUBLE OR NOTHING

Rachel correctly wrote down “who is Eos?”, and made her daily total $22, 200 with a huge wager.

Ray also got the correct answer - and sensing the competition, he had wagered everything.

He doubled his earnings to $26,400, putting him in the lead.

Emily, who was previously ahead and still had enough wagering-power to knock Ray from the top spot, got the answer wrong.

She incorrectly guessed “who is Luna?” and lost her $12,101 wager.

Ray therefore secured his 11th straight win as he enjoys his place as the second super-champion of season 39.
