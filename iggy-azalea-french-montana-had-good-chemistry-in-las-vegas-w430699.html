Iggy Azalea, French Montana Had 'Good Chemistry' in Las Vegas

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, February 8, 2024

New couple alert? Iggy Azalea and French Montana were spotted getting close while partying in Las Vegas on Saturday, July 23, an onlooker tells Us Weekly.

Azalea, 26, and Montana, 31, attended Jennifer Lopez‘s residency show before heading to Jewel Nightclub, where they hung out with friends before the “Booty” rapper performed some of her hits for fans.

“At multiple points, he leaned in to her and swiped his hand across her lower back and shoulder,” the eyewitness says, noting that they had good chemistry. “[They] hung out for a few minutes, laughing, dancing and throwing their hands in the air.”

Azalea later took the stage during the night. She belted out some of her biggest hits, such as “Fancy” and “Black Widow.”

“He was bobbing around during her performance watching the crowd,” the onlooker notes. The pair then left together around 2:30 a.m.

As previously reported, Azalea is newly single after ending her engagement to Nick Young in June. That month, she claimed via Twitter that she caught the L.A. Lakers player, 31, cheating on her at their home. Earlier this month, Young’s ex Keonna Green exclusively confirmed to Us Weekly that she’s pregnant with their second child.

Despite the split, Azalea appeared to be in good spirits in Las Vegas. She opened up about Young and what it’s been like decorating her new place. (Azalea moved out of Young’s L.A. pad in June.)

“It hasn’t been totally fresh but it’s fun mixing it up and doing all the drapes and boring stuff that girls love,” she told reporters on the red carpet. “I renovated the home, so it’s like that weird anxiety when you are doing something but also fun and seeing how it all works out.”

She added: “Change is good.”

Azalea is also concentrating on her music — but don’t hold out for any breakup anthems on her new album, Digital Distortion. She revealed that no tracks will be about their split because she recorded it back in February.
