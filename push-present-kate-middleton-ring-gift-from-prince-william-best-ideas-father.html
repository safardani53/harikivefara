Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, May 23, 2024

GIVING birth is arguably one of the biggest moments of a woman’s life.

And even if you have more than one child, no two births are the same and are special in their own way - so they all deserve marking.

What is a push present?

A push present is the name for a gift given to a woman by her partner, spouse or father of the baby, to congratulate her for giving birth.

Even though some women may have a c-section, a "push" present literally refers to the act of giving birth.

The present is intended to be a thank you and compensation for all the months of hard work that goes into a pregnancy.

Was Kate Middleton's ring a gift from Prince William?

The world was eagerly awaiting the appearance of Kate Middleton at Prince Harry and Meghan Markle's wedding on May 19 2018, her first public appearance since welcoming her third child the month before.

The Duchess gave birth to Prince Louis on April 23.

Royal fans were quick to notice a huge sparkler on her right hand.

The piece of amber jewellery was rumoured to be a "push present" from Prince William.

But it's since been revealed that she'd actually owned the jewel for a decade.

Kate previously wore the ring to the wedding of Lady Rose Windsor and George Gilman in July 2008, as well as her 26th birthday celebrations in January of the same year.

However, William most likely did treat her to something.

The Duke of Cambridge is thought to have given his wife a gift following the birth of each of their children.

After the birth of their first child in 2013, Prince George, William is said to have got his wife a limited-edition teddy from Harrods.

And after Princess Charlotte was born, the second-in-line to the throne is said to have given Kate a pair of earrings worth around £3,000.

The earrings are said to have been designed by Kate’s favourite jeweller, Kiki McDonough, and featured green tourmaline, green amethyst, and diamonds.

What are the best ideas for new fathers?

Push presents are typically expensive pieces of jewellery, but there’s no official rule.

Celebrities are known to splash the cash, with Kim Kardashian posting her request online.

She said: "This pregnancy, I would love a Lorraine Schwartz diamond choker, like the ones I've worn before to the Art + Film Gala. Too much? LOL!"

She was later spotted with the choker.

Website Parents.com details a list of gifts dads can use as inspiration.

Unsurprisingly jewellery features heavily, but you don’t need to spend thousands to show your baby mother you care.

Personalised pieces are quite popular, with engravings of the new baby’s name, or the date they were born, or even the baby’s name in Morse code all popular choices.

Cameras are also seen as a good gift, allowing new parents to capture special moments with their newborn.

Other practical items, which can also be personalised, include blankets quilts.

As new mums need something to wrap their baby in, or will be up late doing night feeds, it’s a a present with purpose.
