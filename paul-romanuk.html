Career

From 1981 to 1984, Romanuk volunteered for the Toronto Marlboros Sunday broadcasts on Ryerson’s campus radio station, CKLN-FM. He began his career as a stats and technical equipment man for play-by-play announcer Michael Landsberg.

Romanuk’s first paid broadcasting job came in 1983, while he was still a Ryerson undergraduate when he was hired as a color analyst for Oshawa Generals junior hockey on CKAR 1350 AM radio alongside play-by-play man Mike Inglis. By 1984, Inglis had departed, and Romanuk took over as play-by-play announcer, a position he held until 1987.

Following graduation, Romanuk worked at CBC Sports as a stats assistant and runner for Hockey Night in Canada play-by-play announcer Bob Cole, color analyst Harry Neale, and studio host Dave Hodge. Romanuk began his career in 1985 as an editorial assistant in the newsroom of TSN, a new all-sports cable channel.
Romanuk was eventually hired by Global Television Network to host the OHL Game of the Week show during the 1986-87 season. In October 1987, Romanuk joined TSN as a full-time commentator and reporter.

Similarly, he was the NHL’s play-by-play announcer on TSN, replacing Jim Hughson. Hughson left TSN in 1994–95, and Romanuk became the network’s lead NHL play-by-play announcer from 1994–95 to 1997–98.

More On Career

From 1998 to 2001, he was also the English-language television face of the Montreal Canadiens’ regional broadcasts on TSN. Romanuk accepted an offer to host a morning drive show on The Team 1050, a newly rebranded AM station in Toronto, in the spring of 2001, after fourteen years at TSN.

Romanuk has reported on numerous international sporting events for Canada’s CBC, CTV, TSN, and Sportsnet, including play-by-play coverage of triathlon and weightlifting at the 2012 Summer Olympics in London.

He also provided voice-over work for the International Ice Hockey Federation, commentary for the Champions Hockey League in 2008-09, and emceed the 2012 Hockey Forum in Barcelona and the 2014 CHL draw in Minsk. He is also a well-known author of children’s books, specializing in hockey-themed books.
