Donald Trump gorilla channel spoof fools internet after Fire and Fury parody claims US Preside

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, March 14, 2024

DONALD Trump has been accused of many things in the past 24 hours - and being a huge fan of gorillas is just one of them.

A spoof Twitter account made the most of the swarm of claims against Trump in the bombshell Fire and Fury book to pretend the US President watches gorillas fighting all day on a dedicated channel.

The viral parody, laid out to look like an excerpt from Michael Wolff's tell-all book, fooled the internet and even a few US pundits.

It claimed: "On his first night in the White House, President Trump complained that the TV in his bedroom was broken, because it didn’t have ‘the gorilla channel’.

"To appease Trump, White House staff compiled a number of gorilla documentaries into a makeshift gorilla channel, broadcast into Trump’s bedroom from a hastily-constructed transmission tower on the South Lawn."

It then added that Trump was unhappy with the channel because "the gorillas aren't fighting".

The faux excerpt became even more outlandish, eventually ending the parody story by quoting an "insider", who said: "He kneels in front of the TV, with his face about four inches from the screen, and says encouraging things to the gorillas, like 'the way you hit that other gorilla was good'. I think he thinks the gorillas can hear him."

The account @pixelatedboat released the screen grab which appeared to be from the book that was released four days early amid much controversy.

The tweet was quickly picked up on Twitter, with some quick to believe the parody.

One person wrote: "I read pretty far in before I realized..."

Another added: "I did not realize it was a joke until I read the comments!! We're so far down the rabbit hole, this seemed completely believable. What's happening to us????"

A third said: "Once I read 17 hours I knew that it was a stretch, LOL. Anyway, how desperately sad that this insane behavior is somewhat believable ?"

MOST READ IN NEWS

Wolff's book is reportedly based on 200 interviews with White House staffers and members of Trump's election campaign team, including Steve Bannon.

The litany of bombshell claims in the book include that First Lady Melania "cried" when Trump won the election.

But the White House has rubbished the claims, saying they were "complete fantasy".
