Maralee Nichols shares rare video of son Theo, 2, in Santa outfit as tot's dad Tristan Thompson 'ref

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, September 1, 2024

MARALEE Nichols has shared a rare clip of the young son she shares with Tristan Thompson, Theo, wearing a Santa outfit.

Tristan, 32, fathered a love child with the ex-mistress during his supposedly exclusive relationship with Khloe Kardashian, 39.

Tristan and Khloe share two children together: daughter True, 5, and son Tatum, 1.

When it comes to the son he shares with Maralee, however, Tristan reportedly told the Instagram model that he is uninterested in spending time with Theo and has no intention of getting to know him.

Most recently, Maralee, 33, took to her IG Story to share an adorable clip of Theo from behind as he played with a moving toy train.

As the toy blew bubbles into the air, Theo was seen chasing after it while wearing a full Santa outfit.

WHERE'S TRISTAN?

Most recently, Maralee took to the platform to celebrate her son's 2nd birthday.

However, the NBA star remained quiet on that day, only continuing to show love for his other children.

Aside from the kids he shares with Maralee and Khloe, Tristan is a father to a six-year-old son, Prince, with his ex, Jordan Craig, 31.

In another Story post earlier this month, Maralee shared a throwback photo of her pregnant body carried in the air by a silk curtain.

She captioned the post: "Can't believe my baby is two today."

"Spending the past 365 days with you have been the best days of my life."

She concluded: "You are my greatest blessing, my world, my heart in human form. God knew I needed you."

However, Tristan did not appear to show the same love for his son's special day.

HUSH MONEY

Maralee and Tristan had a one-night stand in March 2021, when the basketball player was still exclusive with Khloe.

The fitness model had to sue Tristan for child support payments, leading their affair to come to light.

According to legal documents from August 2022, the sports star offered to pay Maralee $75,000 in hush money instead of paying monthly child support.

The athlete allegedly told Theo's mother that she would be "better off" with $75,000 because she would not get much from him for child support.

When the two eventually settled their paternity case, the court ordered the basketball star to pay about $9,500 per month in child support.
